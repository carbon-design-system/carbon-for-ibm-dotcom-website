Carbon for IBM.com is an open source design system for IBM online experiences. It is for the community of designers, developers, and anyone involved in the making of marketing and editorial experiences on IBM.com.

</PageDescription>

## Introduction
The goals of Carbon for IBM.com includes improving UI consistency and quality, while making the design and development process more efficient and focused. We also establish a shared vocabulary between designer and developer, providing clear and discoverable guidance around design and development best practices.

_Please note that this page is part of a controlled adoption of the new Carbon Design System and is subject to change. Presently, it is only for DGC squads building IBM.com services and experiences._

## What is in Carbon for IBM.com?
* Carbon for IBM.com components and patterns
* Web-specific implementation guidelines 
* Sketch design kit 
* Examples and resources
* Built-in metrics and accessibility compliance
* Optimized code

## Guiding principles of Carbon for IBM.com
* **Open:** It is a design system that is a distributed effort, guided by the principles of the open source movement. Its users are also its makers while everyone is encouraged to contribute.
* **Inclusive:** Designed and built to be accessible to all regardless of ability or situation.
* **Flexible:** The system’s modularity ensures maximum flexibility in execution. Its components are designed to work seamlessly with each other, in the combination that best suits the needs of the user.
* **Users first:** Using rigorous research into user’s needs and actions, Carbon for IBM.com is devoted to real people and their demands.
* **Consistency:** Based on the IBM Design Language, every element and component of Carbon for IBM.com was designed from the ground up to work elegantly together, to ensure consistent and cohesive user experiences.
